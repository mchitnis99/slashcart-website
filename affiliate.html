Affiliate Disclosure

Last updated: May 23, 2026

The short version: When you click a "Buy" link on SlashCart and make a purchase, we may earn a small commission from Amazon or Walmart. This doesn't cost you anything extra — and it helps us keep SlashCart free.

What This Means

SlashCart participates in affiliate advertising programs designed to provide a means for us to earn fees by linking to retailer websites. When you click a product link on SlashCart and complete a purchase, we may receive a commission from the retailer.

Our Affiliate Partnerships

  • Amazon Associates Program — SlashCart is a participant in the Amazon Services LLC Associates Program, an affiliate advertising program designed to provide a means for sites to earn advertising fees by advertising and linking to Amazon.com. We may earn 1–4% commission on qualifying purchases made within 24 hours of clicking our links.
  • Walmart Affiliate Program — We participate in the Walmart affiliate program through Impact.com. We may earn commission on qualifying purchases made within 3 days of clicking our links.

Our Commitment to You

Affiliate commissions do not influence our price comparisons or recommendations. SlashCart always shows you the actual lowest price we find — we never artificially favor one retailer over another to earn a higher commission.

Our goal is simple: show you the best price. If Walmart is cheaper, we say Walmart. If Amazon is cheaper, we say Amazon. The affiliate relationship is how we keep the service free — not how we make recommendations.

No Extra Cost to You

Clicking affiliate links on SlashCart does not increase the price you pay. The commission comes from the retailer's marketing budget, not from you.

FTC Compliance

This disclosure is made in accordance with the Federal Trade Commission's guidelines on endorsements and testimonials in advertising (16 CFR Part 255). We are required to disclose any material connection between us and the products or services we recommend.
